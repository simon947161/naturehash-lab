# NatureHash Concept Paper v0.1

## Working Title

NatureHash: A Conceptual Framework for Ecological State Proof in the Quantum-AI Era

## Subtitle

From Cryptographic Hash to Cosmic Ecological Fingerprint

## Abstract Draft

NatureHash is proposed as a conceptual framework for ecological state proof.

Traditional blockchain systems use cryptographic hashes to support digital transactions, block histories, state roots, and data integrity.

However, future intelligent systems may require trust infrastructure that can verify not only digital events, but also natural states across location, time, observation, evidence, ecological meaning, and governance context.

NatureHash begins from the hypothesis that natural-world evidence can be structured into verifiable ecological state fingerprints.

This paper does not propose a final algorithm.

Instead, it defines the civilisational need, conceptual lineage, possible architecture, research questions, and integration pathways for future NatureHash development.

## Initial Research Questions

- What counts as a natural state?
- How should location and time be encoded?
- How should observation sources be weighted?
- How should confidence and uncertainty be represented?
- How can ecological meaning be preserved rather than flattened?
- Which parts of the evidence should be public, private, or protected?
- How can NatureHash connect with climate governance, carbon accounting, biodiversity, water, energy, and regional resilience?
- What lessons can be inherited from Bitcoin, Ethereum, Merkle proofs, DAG chains, location proofs, digital twins, and post-quantum trust research?
